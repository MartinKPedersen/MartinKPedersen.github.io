The first observation we can make is that all districts has had an overall increase in population - this is visualized by the radius of each circle. This is of course to be expected. The second and properly more interesting observation is almost every district has increased in number of high-income citizens. This is clearly visible because the y-axis effectively doubles its max value.

Let us investigate this further. In the following bar diagram the development of high-, medium-, and low-income citizens is displayed throughout our data timeframe. Try changing the district to see its particular development and mouse over bars to get precise data information.

Select district:




While the previous scatterplot gave us an understanding of an overarching trend this grouped bar chart delivers a much deeper understanding. Look at the development of Vest Amager it is quite apparent that this district has had a drastic increase in high-income citizens. This corresponds well with how this district has undergone major improvements over the last decade. Furthermore we see a that the rise in high-income citizens spikes at 2010 while the amount of medium-income citizens lowers. This could indicate a trend towards Copenhagen becoming more attractive for wealthier citizens and averagely paid citizens moving to the suburbs. As a final remark to this chart it is notable that each district follows a similar development pattern in high-, medium- and low-income citizens. There is however another parameter which could confound this statement, namely the age of citizens living in specific areas. Young people tend to earn less money, so if a particular district has been increasingly popular amongst the youth of Copenhagen this could explain its socio economic development. Let us have a look at the distribution of age groups in each district in 1997 and 2013. Should the visualization malfunction simply refresh the page.

//STACKED BAR CHART //

The trend we notice is an overall slight decrease in young people, which is also something to be expected since we in our current society live longer. The largest change happens in the district of Valby where we see a larger increase in the young population. Going back to our bar chart we notice that Valby actually is one of the districts with the lowest increase in high-income citizens which could indicate a correlation.

In order for us to evaluate whether the ancestry of citizens has had any influence on this trend let's zoom in on two interesting districts: Vest Amager due to it being the one with the greatest increase in high-income citizens and Bispebjerg due to it being the one with the greatest increase in low-income citizens.
